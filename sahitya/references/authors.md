# Sanskrit Authors

## Major Poets (Chronological)

### वाल्मीकिः (Valmiki) — Legendary

**Title**: आदिकवि (First Poet)
**Work**: रामायणम् (24,000 shlokas, 7 kandas)
**Significance**: Invented the shloka meter after witnessing a hunter kill a crane

> मा निषाद प्रतिष्ठां त्वमगमः शाश्वतीः समाः।
> यत्क्रौञ्चमिथुनादेकमवधीः काममोहितम्॥

**Style**: Simple, direct, emotional depth

---

### व्यासः (Vyasa) — Legendary

**Title**: वेदव्यासः
**Works**:
- महाभारतम् (100,000 shlokas)
- पुराणाः (18 Mahapuranas)
- ब्रह्मसूत्राणि

**Significance**: Classified the Vedas; composed the "fifth Veda" (Mahabharata)

---

### भासः (Bhasa) — c. 3rd-4th century CE

**Works**: 13 plays rediscovered in 1912
**Style**: Direct, dramatic, less ornate than later poets
**Notable plays**: स्वप्नवासवदत्तम्, ऊरुभङ्गम्, कर्णभारम्

**Legacy**: Influenced Kalidasa; praised in Kalidasa's prologue

---

### कालिदासः (Kalidasa) — c. 4th-5th century CE

**Title**: कविकुलगुरु (Teacher of Poets)
**Court**: Possibly Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya)

| Work | Genre |
|------|-------|
| रघुवंशम् | महाकाव्य |
| कुमारसम्भवम् | महाकाव्य |
| मेघदूतम् | खण्डकाव्य |
| ऋतुसंहारम् | खण्डकाव्य |
| अभिज्ञानशाकुन्तलम् | नाटक |
| विक्रमोर्वशीयम् | नाटक |
| मालविकाग्निमित्रम् | नाटक |

**Style characteristics**:
- माधुर्य (sweetness)
- Mastery of उपमा (simile)
- Nature imagery (especially seasons, mountains, rivers)
- Tender शृङ्गार
- Psychological depth

**Famous assessment**:
> उपमा कालिदासस्य — "Similes belong to Kalidasa"

---

### शूद्रकः (Shudraka) — c. 4th century CE

**Work**: मृच्छकटिकम् (The Little Clay Cart)
**Style**: Social realism; prose and verse mixed
**Significance**: Only major drama with poor Brahmin hero; gritty urban setting

---

### भर्तृहरिः (Bhartrhari) — c. 5th century CE

**Works**:
- शृङ्गारशतकम् (100 verses on love)
- नीतिशतकम् (100 verses on ethics)
- वैराग्यशतकम् (100 verses on renunciation)
- वाक्यपदीयम् (grammar philosophy)

**Style**: Epigrammatic, philosophical, worldly wisdom
**Legend**: Alternated between kingship and renunciation multiple times

---

### भारविः (Bharavi) — c. 6th century CE

**Work**: किरातार्जुनीयम् (18 sargas)
**Style**: अर्थगौरव (depth of meaning)
- Dense, complex syntax
- Philosophical passages
- Virtuoso metrical displays (Sarga 15)

**Assessment**:
> भारवेरर्थगौरवम् — "Depth of meaning belongs to Bharavi"

---

### दण्डी (Dandin) — c. 6th-7th century CE

**Works**:
- दशकुमारचरितम् (prose romance)
- काव्यादर्शः (poetics treatise)

**Style**: Prose master; वैदर्भी style (sweet, flowing)
**Significance**: Defined mahakavya characteristics

> पदलालित्यं दण्डिनः — "Word-grace belongs to Dandin"

---

### बाणभट्टः (Banabhatta) — c. 7th century CE

**Court**: Harshavardhana of Kanauj

**Works**:
- हर्षचरितम् (historical biography)
- कादम्बरी (world's first novel)

**Style**:
- गौडी style (elaborate, ornate)
- Long compound words
- Complex descriptions
- Rich vocabulary

**Famous quote** (from Kadambari):
> अपूर्वः कोऽपि कोशो विद्यमानः कुत्रापि — "Some unprecedented treasure exists somewhere"

---

### माघः (Magha) — c. 7th century CE

**Work**: शिशुपालवधम् (20 sargas)
**Style**: Combined all three gunas:
- Kalidasa's sweetness
- Bharavi's depth
- Dandin's word-grace

> माघे सन्ति त्रयो गुणाः — "Magha has all three qualities"

**Famous for**: Description mastery; sarga 4 (night/moon) particularly celebrated

---

### भवभूतिः (Bhavabhuti) — c. 8th century CE

**Court**: Yashovarman of Kanauj

**Works**:
- उत्तररामचरितम् (7 acts)
- मालतीमाधवम् (10 acts)
- महावीरचरितम् (7 acts)

**Style**:
- करुण रस master
- Philosophical depth
- Grand language (ओजस्)
- Less sweet than Kalidasa, more powerful

**Self-assessment**:
> ये नाम केचिदिह नः प्रथयन्त्यवज्ञाम्...
> कालो ह्ययं निरवधिर्विपुला च पृथ्वी।

"Those who despise us here... Time is infinite, the earth vast."

---

### श्रीहर्षः (Shriharsha) — 12th century CE

**Court**: Gahadavala dynasty

**Work**: नैषधीयचरितम् (22 sargas)
**Style**:
- Most difficult of the five mahakavyas
- Complex compounds
- Philosophical digressions (esp. Vedanta)
- Double entendres

---

### जयदेवः (Jayadeva) — 12th century CE

**Court**: Lakshmana Sena of Bengal

**Work**: गीतगोविन्दम् (12 sargas, 24 songs)
**Style**:
- Musical (each song has a raga/tala)
- Intense भक्ति शृङ्गार
- Radha-Krishna love as divine metaphor
- Ashtapadis (eight-stanza songs)

**Significance**: Bridge between classical and devotional; influenced all later Krishna poetry

---

## Comparative Styles

| Poet | Primary Quality | Best For |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| कालिदास | माधुर्य (sweetness) | उपमा, nature |
| भारवि | अर्थगौरव (depth) | Philosophy, gravitas |
| दण्डी | पदलालित्य (word-grace) | Prose elegance |
| माघ | सर्वगुण (all qualities) | Technical virtuosity |
| बाण | गौडी (ornate) | Long compounds |
| भवभूति | ओजस् (power) | करुण रस |

## Traditional Rankings

**Kalidasa praised above all**:
> पुरा कवीनां गणनाप्रसङ्गे कनिष्ठिकाधिष्ठितकालिदासा।
> अद्यापि तत्तुल्यकवेरभावात् अनामिका सार्थवती बभूव॥

"When counting poets, Kalidasa took the little finger (first).
To this day, the ring finger has meaning (remains unnamed), for no equal has arisen."

## Alankarikas (Theorists)

| Author | Work | Contribution |
|--------|------|--------------|
| भरत | नाट्यशास्त्र | Rasa theory foundation |
| भामह | काव्यालङ्कार | Alankara (figures of speech) |
| दण्डी | काव्यादर्श | Marga (style), Guna |
| वामन | काव्यालङ्कारसूत्र | Riti (style as soul) |
| आनन्दवर्धन | ध्वन्यालोक | Dhvani (suggestion) |
| अभिनवगुप्त | लोचन | Commentary on Dhvanyaloka |
| मम्मट | काव्यप्रकाश | Comprehensive synthesis |
| विश्वनाथ | साहित्यदर्पण | Rasa as soul of kavya |
