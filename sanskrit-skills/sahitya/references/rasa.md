# Rasa Theory (Sanskrit Aesthetics)

## Overview

**Rasa** (रस) = aesthetic flavor, emotional essence, the relishable experience of poetry

The theory originates in Bharata's **नाट्यशास्त्र** and was developed by later theorists.

> विभावानुभावव्यभिचारिसंयोगाद्रसनिष्पत्तिः
> "Rasa arises from the combination of vibhava, anubhava, and vyabhicharibhava"
> — Bharata

## The Nine Rasas (नवरस)

### Primary Eight (Bharata)

| # | Rasa | English | स्थायिभाव | Color | Deity |
|---|------|---------|-----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | शृङ्गार | Erotic | रति (love) | श्याम (dark) | विष्णु |
| 2 | हास्य | Comic | हास (mirth) | सित (white) | प्रमथ |
| 3 | करुण | Pathetic | शोक (sorrow) | कपोत (grey) | यम |
| 4 | रौद्र | Furious | क्रोध (anger) | रक्त (red) | रुद्र |
| 5 | वीर | Heroic | उत्साह (energy) | गौर (golden) | इन्द्र |
| 6 | भयानक | Terrible | भय (fear) | कृष्ण (black) | काल |
| 7 | बीभत्स | Odious | जुगुप्सा (disgust) | नील (blue) | महाकाल |
| 8 | अद्भुत | Marvelous | विस्मय (wonder) | पीत (yellow) | ब्रह्मा |

### Ninth Rasa (added by Abhinavagupta)

| 9 | शान्त | Peaceful | शम/निर्वेद (tranquility) | स्फटिक (crystal) | नारायण |

## Components of Rasa

### 1. विभाव (Vibhava) — Determinants

Causes that stimulate emotion

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **आलम्बन** | Object of emotion | The beloved in शृङ्गार |
| **उद्दीपन** | Excitants | Moonlight, spring, flowers |

### 2. अनुभाव (Anubhava) — Consequents

Physical expressions of emotion

| Example |
|---------|
| Smiles, glances, gestures |
| Speech, movement |
| Tears, trembling |

## Shringara Rasa (Detailed)

The primary rasa, often called **रसराज** (king of rasas).

### Two Types

| Type | Sanskrit | State |
|------|----------|-------|
| सम्भोग | Sambhoga | Union with beloved |
| विप्रलम्भ | Vipralambha | Separation from beloved |

### Vipralambha Subtypes

| Type | Cause |
|------|-------|
| पूर्वानुराग | Before meeting |
| मान | Jealous anger |
| प्रवास | Physical separation |
| करुणावेश | Apparent death |

### 10 States of Love (दशा)

1. अभिलाष — Longing
2. चिन्ता — Anxiety
3. स्मृति — Remembrance
4. गुणकथन — Praising qualities
5. उद्वेग — Distress
6. प्रलाप — Delirium
7. उन्माद — Madness
8. व्याधि — Sickness
9. जडता — Stupor
10. मरण — Death

## Schools of Poetics

### 1. अलङ्कार School (Bhamaha, Dandin)

**Kavya's soul**: अलङ्कार (figures of speech)

> काव्यशोभाकरान् धर्मान् अलङ्कारान् प्रचक्षते
> "Alankaras are the beauty-making elements of kavya"

### 2. रीति School (Vamana)

**Kavya's soul**: रीति (style)

Three styles:
- वैदर्भी — Sweet, flowing, few compounds
- गौडी — Grand, ornate, long compounds
- पाञ्चाली — Mixed

### 3. रस School (Bharata → Abhinavagupta)

**Kavya's soul**: रस (aesthetic emotion)

> रसात्मकं वाक्यं काव्यम्
> "Poetry is language ensouled with rasa"
> — Vishvanatha

### 4. ध्वनि School (Anandavardhana)

**Kavya's soul**: ध्वनि (suggestion)

Three levels of meaning:
1. **वाच्य** — Literal/denoted
2. **लक्ष्य** — Indicated/implied
3. **व्यङ्ग्य** — Suggested (highest)

> काव्यस्यात्मा ध्वनिः
> "Dhvani is the soul of poetry"

Types of Dhvani:
- **वस्तुध्वनि** — Suggested matter
- **अलङ्कारध्वनि** — Suggested figure
- **रसध्वनि** — Suggested rasa (highest)

### 5. वक्रोक्ति School (Kuntaka)

**Kavya's soul**: वक्रोक्ति (oblique expression)

Poetry differs from ordinary language through striking/indirect expression.

### 6. औचित्य School (Kshemendra)

**Kavya's soul**: औचित्य (propriety)

Everything in poetry must be appropriate to context—character, situation, rasa.

## Key Concepts

### रसाभास (Rasabhasa)
"Semblance of rasa" — failed or inappropriate rasa evocation

### रससङ्कर (Rasasankara)
Mixture of rasas (generally to be avoided, but skilled poets manage it)

### अङ्गी-अङ्ग (Angi-Anga)
Primary rasa (angi) supported by subordinate rasas (anga)

### विरोधी रस (Virodhi Rasa)
Incompatible rasas:
- शृङ्गार vs वीर (erotic vs heroic)
- हास्य vs करुण (comic vs pathetic)
