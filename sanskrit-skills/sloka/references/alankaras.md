# Sanskrit Alankaras (Figures of Speech)

## Shabdalankaras (Sound-based)

### Anuprasa (अनुप्रास) - Alliteration
Repetition of consonants.
- **Cheka**: Same consonant cluster repeated
- **Vṛtti**: Single consonant repeated
- **Example**: कामं कर्म करोति कः

### Yamaka (यमक) - Punning Repetition
Same syllable sequence with different meanings.
- **Example**: काले कलौ कालकलाकलापम् (kāle = in time; kalau = in Kali age; kāla = death; kalā = arts)

### Shlesha (श्लेष) - Paronomasia
Word with multiple simultaneous meanings.
- **Example**: रामो राजमणिः सदा विजयते (Rama = भगवान् राम or रामचन्द्र)

## Arthalankaras (Meaning-based)

### Upama (उपमा) - Simile
Explicit comparison using इव, यथा, सदृश, तुल्य, etc.
- **Components**: उपमेय (compared), उपमान (standard), साधारण धर्म (common quality), उपमा-वाचक (marker)
- **Example**: मुखं चन्द्र इव शोभते (face shines like the moon)

### Rupaka (रूपक) - Metaphor
Identity stated between compared objects.
- **Example**: मुखचन्द्रः शोभते (the moon-face shines) - face IS moon

### Utpreksha (उत्प्रेक्षा) - Poetic Fancy
Imagining one thing as another using इव, मन्ये, शङ्के.
- **Example**: उन्मीलन्ति इव लोचने (eyes seem to be opening)

### Vyatireka (व्यतिरेक) - Contrast
Upameya shown superior to upamana.
- **Example**: चन्द्रो न तव मुखस्य सदृशः (moon is not equal to your face)

### Atishayokti (अतिशयोक्ति) - Hyperbole
Extreme exaggeration beyond possibility.
- **Example**: तेन रोदिता पृथ्वी पूरिता अश्रुभिः (earth filled with her tears)

### Arthantaranyasa (अर्थान्तरन्यास) - Corroboration
General statement supported by specific example or vice versa.
- **Example**: रत्नं मातुः पादाभ्यां लब्धम् | साधवो हि उत्तमकुलजाः (gem from mother's feet | indeed, the good are born in noble families)

### Dristanta (दृष्टान्त) - Illustration
Parallel example from different domain.
- **Structure**: बिम्ब (image) and प्रतिबिम्ब (reflection)
- **Example**: उदेति सविता ताम्रः... (sun rises red...)

### Svabhavokti (स्वभावोक्ति) - Natural Description
Vivid description of inherent nature without comparison.
- **Example**: Describing a lion's walk, river's flow naturally

### Virodha (विरोध) - Paradox
Apparent contradiction resolved at deeper level.
- **Example**: जीवन्मृतः (living-dead - one who lives meaninglessly)

### Vibhavana (विभावना) - Cause-Effect Reversal
Effect without expected cause, or unexpected cause.
- **Example**: अग्निना दग्धं न तप्तम् (burned by fire but not heated)

### Visheshokti (विशेषोक्ति) - Peculiar Statement
Expected effect doesn't occur despite cause.
- **Example**: चन्द्रोदये तमो न नष्टम् (darkness not dispelled at moonrise)

### Samuccaya (समुच्चय) - Combination
Multiple attributes or actions combined.
- **Example**: सुन्दरी च शीला च विदुषी च (beautiful and virtuous and learned)

### Paryaya (पर्याय) - Sequence
Serial occurrence of attributes/actions.
- **Example**: प्रातः पूजा, मध्याह्ने भोजनम्, सायं विश्रामः

## Ubhayalankaras (Both Sound and Meaning)

### Vakrokti (वक्रोक्ति) - Indirect Expression
Meaning different from literal surface.
- **Shlesha-mula**: Based on double meaning
- **Kaku-mula**: Based on tone/intonation

## Identifying Alankaras

1. Look for comparison markers (इव, यथा) → Upama
2. Look for identity statements (X is Y) → Rupaka
3. Look for sound repetition → Anuprasa/Yamaka
4. Look for double meanings → Shlesha
5. Look for exaggeration → Atishayokti
6. Look for natural vivid description → Svabhavokti
7. Look for logical support structure → Arthantaranyasa
